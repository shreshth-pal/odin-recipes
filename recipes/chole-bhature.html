Chole Bhature

The perfect antidote to insomnia Chole bhature

Chole Bhature is a spicy, tasty and a filling dish. I have eaten this dish at many places in Punjab and Delhi. Each restaurants or dhaba (eateries on highways) have their own flavors and taste in the chickpea curry.

In some places the curry is very spicy, at other places it has tangy taste and the consistency of the curry also varies from slightly thick to semi-dry and dry.

Ingredients

bhatura

  • 2 cup maida
  • 2 tbsp semolina
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/4 tsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp oil and some water

chole

  • 1 cup chana
  • 2 tea bags
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 3 cup water
  • oil
  • chola masala
  • corriander
  • onions
  • tomato puree
  • ghee

Recipe

bhatura

  1. firstly, in a large bowl take 2 cup maida, 2 tbsp rava, 1 tsp sugar, ¼ tsp baking soda, 1 tsp sugar, ½ tsp salt and 2 tbsp oil. mix well.
  2. now add ¼ cup curd and mix well making sure everything is well combined.
  3. knead to the smooth and soft dough without putting much pressure.
  4. roll slightly thick, applying oil to prevent from sticking.
  5. drop the rolled dough into the hot oil.

chole

  1. firstly, in a pressure cooker take soaked chana. i have soaked 1 cup chana in enough water for 8 hours.
  2. add 2 tea bags, ¼ tsp baking soda, 1 tsp salt and 3 cup water.
  3. cover and pressure cook for 5 whistles. if you do not have tea bags, then you can prepare tea decoction and add to cooker.
  4. once the pressure releases, open the cooker and discard the tea bags. keep aside
  5. further add 1½ cup tomato puree and saute until the oil separates. to prepare tomato puree, i have blended 2½ ripened tomatoes in blender without adding water.
